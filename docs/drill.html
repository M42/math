1 Category theory

1.1 Universality and Yoneda

1.1.1 Universal arrow   drill

Definition of universal arrow from \(c\) to \(S\).

  1. Definition

    For any other \(f : c \to Sd\) there exists this unique diagram

    \[\begin{tikzcd} & Sd \\ c \rar[swap]{u}\urar{f} & Sr \uar[swap, dashed]{\exists! S \widetilde f} \end{tikzcd}\]

1.1.2 Yoneda Lemma   drill

Statement of the first Yoneda Lemma.

  1. Statement

    For any covariant \(K\colon D \to \mathsf{Set}\) and \(r \in D\), there is a bijection

    \[ y \colon \mathrm{Nat}(\mathrm{hom}_{D}(r,-), K) \cong Kr \]

    sending any natural transformation \(\alpha \colon \mathrm{hom}_{D}(r,-) \xrightarrow{.} K\) to its image on the identity, \(\alpha_r(\mathsf{id}_r)\).

1.1.3 Yoneda functor   drill

Definition of the Yoneda functor.

  1. Definition

    The Yoneda functor, \(Y \colon D^{op} \to\Sets^{D}\), is defined with the arrow function \[ \left(f \colon s \to r\right) \mapsto \Big(- \circ f \colon \hom_D(r,-) \to \hom_D(s,-)\Big). \] It can be also written as \(Y' \colon D \to\Sets^{D^{op}}\).

1.2 Adjoints, monads and algebras

1.2.1 Unit of an adjunction   drill

Given an adjunction \(F \vdash G\), what signature does the unit have?

  1. Answer

    The unit is \(\eta \colon \mathrm{id} \to G \circ F\).

1.2.2 Counit of an adjunction   drill

Given an adjunction \(F \vdash G\), what signature does the counit have?

  1. Answer

    The counit is \(\epsilon : F \circ G \to \mathrm{id}\).

1.2.3 Monad of an adjunction   drill

Given an adjunction \(F \vdash G\), what monad does it give rise to?

  1. Answer

    The monad \(T := G \circ F\).

1.3 Enriched categories

1.3.1 Preadditive category   drill

Definition of preadditive category

  1. Definition

    Every \(hom(a,b)\) is an abelian group and composition is bilinear

    \[ f \circ (g+h) = f \circ g+f \circ h \qquad (f+g)\circ h = f\circ h+g \circ h \]

1.4 Categorical logic

1.4.1 Cartesian closed category   drill

Define a cartesian closed category using three adjoints.

  1. Definition

    Any category \({\cal C}\) is cartesian closed if and only if there exist right adjoints for the following functors

    \begin{prooftree} \AX$\ast\ \fCenter\to\ast$ \doubleLine \UI$x\ \fCenter\to \top$ \AX$(x,x)\ \fCenter\to (y,z)$ \doubleLine \UI$x\ \fCenter\to y \times z$ \AX$x \times a\ \fCenter\to y$ \doubleLine \UI$x\ \fCenter\to y^a$ \noLine \TIC{} \end{prooftree}

1.4.2 Elementary topos   drill

Define elementary topos with three properties.

  1. Definition
    1. is cartesian closed,
    2. has all finite limits,
    3. has a subobject classifier.

1.4.3 Pullback functor in objects   drill

Given \(f : B \to A\), define the pullback functor \(f^{\ast} : {\cal C}/A \to {\cal C}/B\) in objects.

  1. Definition in objects

    Given any \(c : C \to A\), the pullback functor gives \(f^{\ast}c : f^{\ast}C \to B\) with the following pullback square

    \[\begin{tikzcd} f^{\ast}C \rar{}\drar[phantom, near start, "\ulcorner"] \dar[swap]{f^{\ast}c} & C \dar{c} \\ B\rar{f} & A \end{tikzcd}\]

1.4.4 Pullback functor in morphisms   drill

Given \(f : B \to A\), define the pullback functor \(f^{\ast} : {\cal C}/A \to {\cal C}/B\) on the morphism \(h : C \to D\) defined between \(c : C \to A\) and \(d : D \to A\).

  1. Definition in morphisms

    The morphism \(f^{\ast}h : f^{\ast}C \to f^{\ast}D\) is defined by the universal property of pullbacks

    \[\begin{tikzcd} f^{\ast}C \ar{rrr}\ar[dashed]{dr}{\exists! f^{\ast}h} \ar[bend right,swap]{ddr}{f^{\ast}c} &&& C \ar{dl}{h}\ar[bend left=60]{ddl}{c} \\ & f^{\ast}D \dar[swap]{f^{\ast}d} \rar & D \dar{d} \\ & BÃ± \rar[swap]{f} & A \\ \end{tikzcd}\]

2 Homotopy type theory

2.1 3. Sets and logic

2.1.1 Sets   drill

Definition of sets in type theory.

  1. Definition

    \[ \textsf{isSet}(A) \equiv \prod_{(x,y : A)} \prod_{(p,q : x = y)} p = q. \]

2.1.2 Subtypes   drill

Definition of subtype.

  1. Definition

    For a family of mere propositions \(P : A \to {\cal U}\), we write

    \[ \left\{ x : A\mid P(x) \right\} \equiv \sum_{x:A} P(x) \]

    and call this a subtype of \(A\).

2.1.3 Axiom of choice   drill

Axiom of choice in type theory.

  1. Statement

    \[ \left( \prod_{x:X} \trunc{\sum_{a:A(x)} P(x,a)} \right) \to \trunc{\sum_{(g: \prod_{x:X}A(x))} \prod_{(x:X)} P(x,g(x))}. \]

    for a given \(P : \prod_{x:X} (A(x) \to {\cal U})\).

2.1.4 Decidable type   drill

Definition of decible type.

  1. Definition

    \(A + \neg A\)

2.1.5 Decidable equality   drill

Definition of decidable equality.

  1. Definition

    \[ \prod_{a,b : A} (a = b) + \neg (a = b) \]

2.1.6 Decidable type family   drill

Definition of decidable type family.

  1. Definition

    \[ \prod_{a : A} B(a) + \neg B(a) \]

2.1.7 Contractible type   drill

Definition of contractible type.

  1. Definition

    \[ \isContr(A) :\equiv \sum_{a:A}\prod_{x:A}(a = x) \]

    \(A\) is contractible if there exists a center of contraction \(a\).

2.1.8 Mere propositions and contractibility   drill

Define mere proposition in terms of contractibility.

  1. Definition

    \(A\) is a mere proposition iff for all \(x,y : A\), the type \(x = y\) is contractible.

2.1.9 Hedberg's theorem   drill

Statement of Hedberg's theorem.

  1. Statement

    If a type has decidable equality, it is a set.

2.2 4. Equivalences

2.2.1 Type-theoretical fiber   drill

Definition of fiber of \(f : A \to B\) over \(b : B\) in HoTT.

  1. Definition

    \[ \fib_f(b) :\equiv \sum_{a:A}(f(a) = b). \]

3 Topology

3.1 Covering space   drill

Definition of covering space.

3.1.1 Definition

A continuous surjective \(p : E \to B\) such that every \(b \in B\) has a neighborhood \(V\) such that \(p^{-1}(V)\) is an open partition \(\{V_n\}\) of slices, every one of them isomorphic to \(V_b\).

3.2 Topological retraction   drill

Definition of topological retraction.

3.2.1 Definition

An inclusion \(i : A \subseteq X\) with a continuous map \(r : X \to A\) such that \(r \circ i = \mathsf{id}\).

3.3 Examples

3.3.1 Are the open and closed intervals homeomorphic?   drill

Prove or disprove: are the open and closed intervals homeomorphic?

  1. Answer

    No, [0,1] is compact and (0,1) is not. Compactness is preserved by homeomorphisms.

  2. Answer 2

    We can take a point on both intervals and see that only one of them has to be connected.

4 Algebra

4.1 Exponentiation of versors   drill

Given a quaternion \(q\), how we define and compute \(\mathrm{exp}(q)\)?

4.1.1 Definition

We define the exponentiation using Taylor Series

\[\exp(q) = 1 + q + \frac{q^2}{2!} + \frac{q^3}{3!} + \dots + \frac{q^n}{n!} + \cdots\ .\]

4.1.2 Computation

If we write any quaternion as a versor, \(q = \cos\theta + v\sin\theta\), we can use that \(v\) is unitary, \(v^2 = -1\), to compute the Euler's formula for quaternions

\[q = \exp(\theta v) = \cos \theta + v \sin \theta,\]

and then

\[q^t = \exp(t \theta v) = \cos (t \theta) + v \sin (t \theta).\]

4.2 Snake lemma   drill

Given a morphism of short exact sequences as

\[\begin{tikzcd} & A \rar{f}\dar{a} & B \rar{g}\dar{b} & C \rar\dar{c} & 0 \\ 0 \rar & A' \rar{f'} & B' \rar{g'} & C' \\ \end{tikzcd}\]

what does the snake lemma provide?

4.2.1 Answer

There exists a morphism \(\delta \colon \operatorname{ker} c \to \operatorname{coker} a\) such that the following sequence is exact

\[\begin{tikzcd} 0 \rar & \mathrm{ker}(a) \rar{f} & \mathrm{ker}(b) \rar{g} & \mathrm{ker}(c) \arrow[out = 0,in =180,swap]{dll}{\delta} \\& \mathrm{coker}(a) \rar{f'} & \mathrm{coker}(b) \rar{g'} & \mathrm{coker}(c) \rar & 0 \end{tikzcd}\]

Diagramatically,

\[ \begin{tikzcd} & 0 \dar & 0 \dar & 0 \dar & \\ 0 \rar & ker(a) \rar \dar & ker(b) \rar \dar & ker(c) \dar \ar[out=355, in=175,looseness=1, overlay, swap]{dddll}{\delta} & \\ & A \rar{f} \dar{a} & B \rar{g} \dar{b} & C \rar \dar{c} & 0 \\ 0 \rar & A' \rar{f'} \dar & B' \rar{g'} \dar & C' \dar & \\ & coker(a) \rar \dar & coker(b) \rar \dar & coker(c) \rar \dar & 0 \\ & 0 & 0 & 0 & \end{tikzcd} \]

5 First-order logic

5.1 Definition of validity   drill

When is a sentence \(\varphi\) valid?

5.1.1 Answer

\(\varphi\) is valid, written \(\models \varphi\) iff \(\model \models \varphi\) for every structure \(\model\). Every structure satisfies the sentence.

5.2 Definition of soundness   drill

When is a proof system sound?

5.2.1 Answer

When \(\Gamma \vdash \Phi\) implies \(\Gamma \models \Phi\).

You cannot prove anything that is wrong.

Where

  • \(\Gamma \vdash \Phi\) is logical entailment with the inference rules of the system;
  • \(\Gamma \models \Phi\), is implication in the desired semantics; every structure satisfies it.

5.3 Definition of completeness   drill

When is a proof system complete?

5.3.1 Answer

When \(\Gamma \models \Phi\) implies \(\Gamma \vdash \Phi\).

You can prove everything that is true.

Where

  • \(\Gamma \vdash \Phi\) is logical entailment with the inference rules of the system;
  • \(\Gamma \models \Phi\), is implication in the desired semantics; every structure satisfies it.

Defined produces a transparent definition whereas Qed provides an opaque one.
